How can I withdraw my exam entry before the standard entry closing date? #199

You can withdraw your entry up until the standard entry closing date for the relevant exam session. To withdraw your exam entry:

  • Login to myACCA

  • On the left select My Qualification (if using a mobile device select Menu from the top left corner)

  • Under Exams click on the red Book an Exam button

  • The exam entry planner should now open

  • Look for the red button which says view exam entry and click this and you should see your booking

  • You should then be able to cancel your booking.

Within 48 hours, you'll receive an email confirming the withdrawal of the exam(s).

Any fees paid will be credited back to your myACCA and can be used towards your next exam booking.


What evidence is required for exam withdrawal due to illness? #207

To withdraw from an exam due to illness, please provide an officially stamped medical letter or report from a doctor/GP/hospital/medical centre. 

The documents should include the date(s) of illness that prevented you from attending the exam. 

If the documents are in a language other than English, we require both the original language documents and an official translation to English (signed and stamped by the translator). Please make sure to contact us before the withdrawal deadline date with the relevant documentation.


How can I contact ACCA to withdraw from an exam due to illness? #207

To withdraw from an exam due to illness, please use the withdrawal option on the Contact Us form to speak to a representative via webchat. We can review your case and give you an update within minutes.


What happens if my request for exam withdrawal due to illness is granted? #207

If your request for exam withdrawal due to illness is granted, the fees will be returned to your myACCA account to use towards payment for the next exam you take, or we can arrange to refund the exam fee to your payment card.


What happens when my exam withdrawal request is granted? #208

When your exam withdrawal request is granted, your fee will be credited to your myACCA account, and you should not attend the exam under any circumstances. If you'd like to enter the next exam session, you can do so via your myACCA account. Please make sure you're aware of the booking deadline dates.


What if I attend the exam after my withdrawal request is granted? #208

If you attend the exam after your withdrawal request is granted, you will be liable to pay additional fees.


Can I take my exam remotely in the future if I am unwell? #208

Yes, you might be able to take your exam remotely in the future if you are unwell. Visit the ACCA Global website for more information on our remote exams.


What if my condition affects my ability to sit exams moving forward? #208

If your condition may affect your ability to sit exams moving forward, please visit the website for information on requesting additional support during your examinations. Best of luck with your studies!


What if my exam withdrawal request has been rejected? #209

We're sorry to hear that your request has been rejected. We can only process requests for serious extenuating circumstances supported by documentary evidence.


What will happen to my exam fee? #209

Unfortunately, as your request has been rejected, your exam fee cannot be refunded.


What if I want to enter for this exam in the next session? #209

You can enter the exam in the next session once the exam results are released.


Will my absence from the exam be recorded? #209

Yes, as you didn't attend the exam, you'll be marked as absent on your transcript and myACCA account.


Where can I find more information about exam cancellation? #209

You can find more information about exam cancellation in the Frequently Asked Questions section on the ACCA Global website.


What should I do if I couldn't attend my exam due to exceptional reasons? #273

Use the withdrawal option on the contact us form to speak to a representative via webchat.


What documents do I need to provide for consideration? #273

Relevant supporting documents such as an official medical letter or report from a doctor / GP / hospital / medical centre. These documents must detail the date(s) of illness that prevented you from attending the exam or death certificate.

If the documents are in a language other than English, a copy of the official translation to English is required. 


When is the deadline to submit the documents for the withdrawal request? #273

The documents must be received by XXXWITHDRAWAL DEADLINE DATE for the XXX session.


What happens if my withdrawal request is granted? #273

The fees will be returned to your myACCA account to use towards payment for the next exam you take or we can arrange to refund the exam fee to your payment card.


What kind of circumstances are considered for exam withdrawal? #273

Exam withdrawals will only be considered under the most extenuating circumstances and were supported by documentary evidence. Minor illnesses or workload issues, for example, can’t be considered.


Where can I find further information on exams? #273

For further information, please visit our students' FAQs on the exams page.


Why was my exam refund request rejected? #1406

We're sorry to inform you that we're unable to offer a refund of your exam fee.


Can you explain the reason behind the rejection of my request? #1406

Your exam fee is used to secure the arrangements, resources and costs involved in the exams process, and we can only consider the most extenuating circumstances concerning absence from exams such as critical illness or bereavement. We conducted a full and thorough review of your request and the supporting documentary evidence submitted.


Is there anything that I can do about this decision? #1406

We'd like to assure you that we've applied the correct procedures in reaching this decision, and we can't reconsider the request. We kindly request you to accept this decision as final.


What information do I need to provide to withdraw my exam due to a bereavement? #1742

We're very sorry for your loss. To withdraw your exam due to bereavement, you will need to provide evidence of your circumstances. Please use the withdrawal option on the contact us form to speak to a representative via webchat, where we can review your case and provide further assistance.


What happens if the documents I submit are not in English? #1742

If the documents are issued in a language other than English, we require a copy of the original document(s) as well as a copy of the official translation to English, signed and stamped by the translator.


How soon can I expect to receive an update on my case? #1742

We understand this is a difficult time for you, and we will review your case as soon as possible. Using our Contact Us page and webchat option, we can review your case and give you an update within minutes. We hope you appreciate that we must adhere to the policy when reviewing all exam withdrawal requests.


What should I do if my RI exam has been withdrawn due to issues experienced? #3460

We apologize for the inconvenience caused. Your exam entry has been withdrawn and the fee paid has been credited to your myACCA account. The booking window is open for the XXXX exam session. Please note that the deadline is XXX and closes on XX at midnight UK time.


What are the important system checks for RI exams? #3460

You must pass the mandatory system test (MST) from ACCA’s Exam Planner ahead of starting the check-in process on exam day. You'll be prompted to run the test when you book your exam(s) and before starting your exam check-in process. Please complete another MST if a software update is performed on the device you intend to use for the exam. View minimum requirements.


What should I do if I face technical issues during the RI exam? #3460

The most frequent causes of technical issues with online proctored exams are testing on a work computer, an unreliable internet connection, applications running in the background, and camera or mic settings being disabled or restricted privacy settings. Please contact Pearson VUE if you have any issues with your system test. ACCA Connect cannot assist with any system test technical issues.


What should I do if my documents are issued in a language other than English? #3460

Please provide a copy of the original language document(s) and a copy of the official translation to English (signed and stamped by the translator). This must be received by XXX for the XXX session. Please use the withdrawal option on the contact us form to speak to a representative via webchat to help us process your request quickly.


What are my options if I experienced technical issues during my RI exam session? #3488

You can either rebook for week 2 or withdraw from the exam and receive credit for your exam fees.


When can I rebook for week 2? #3488

The rebook option will be available 60 minutes after the exam was scheduled to start and 48 hours before the exam in the contingency week.


What should I do if the exam is not available within the contingency week? #3488

You may apply for an exam withdrawal if you meet the eligibility criteria.


What are the eligibility criteria for exam withdrawal? #3488

You must have attempted to check in for your exam, experienced a technical issue during your exam which impacted your ability to complete it, and successfully passed the mandatory system test via Exam Planner within the set timeframe.


Where can I find more information on post-exam options for remote exams? #3488

You can visit the website for more information and watch our step-by-step video guidance for support on withdrawing your exam.


When can I withdraw my RI exam if the rebook option is available during the contingency week? #3782

You can only withdraw your exam once the rebook contingency exam date has passed (subject to meeting withdrawal eligibility criteria). Check the timetable for contingency exam dates and the withdrawal option will appear on the Exam Planner after this date until XX. You need to have attempted to check in, experienced technical issues during the exam, and passed the mandatory system test within the set timeframe to be eligible for a withdrawal.


Why am I unable to withdraw my RI exam if the rebook option is not available during the contingency week or the contingency date has passed? #3782

Unfortunately, you do not meet the eligibility criteria to withdraw and receive an exam credit. To be eligible, you need to have attempted to check in, experienced technical issues during the exam, and passed the mandatory system test within the set timeframe. You were notified of these criteria in our communications on the website, Insession support emails and support webinars.


Why can't I withdraw from my exam? #3876

We're sorry to inform you that we cannot process your exam withdrawal request at this time. Our policy for withdrawals has returned to the standard policy where withdrawals will only be considered for serious extenuating circumstances and supported by documentary evidence.


What if I don't attend the exam? #3876

If you don't attend the exam, you'll be marked as absent on your myACCA transcript.


Can I enter the exam in the next session? #3876

Yes, you can enter the exam in the next session once the exam results have been released.


Where can I find more information about exam cancellation? #3876

You can find more information about exam cancellation on our Frequently Asked Questions page on our website.


What if I feel my performance was impacted during the exam due to my current situation? #3876

If you're able to attend the exam but feel that your performance was impacted, you can submit this information via our Mitigating Circumstances platform on myACCA for consideration during the marking process.


Will I receive feedback on my exam experience? #3876

Yes, you will receive detailed personal feedback on your exam experience to help your understanding of your exam mark.

I want to re-register as an ACCA student, what do I need to do? #1348

Great news! We're happy to have you back. To reactivate your account, you'll need to call ACCA Connect at +44(0)141 582 2000 to make a payment or reply to this message if you'd like to proceed with temporary reactivation to make payments online.


Why was my account suspended? #1348

Your account was suspended for (REASON).


What is the outstanding balance on my account? #1348

At the time of removal, the outstanding balance on your account was XXX GBP.


How much is the re-registration fee? #1348

The re-registration fee is XXX GBP.


How can I make a payment? #1348

You can make a payment through bank transfer using the details provided below. Make sure to use your ACCA registration number as the payment reference and send a remittance/allocation list to ACCABankTransfer@accaglobal.com to avoid any payment issues.


Can I pay online? #1348

Yes, we can reactivate your account temporarily to allow you to make payments online. Just let us know by replying to this chat.


What are the exam entry closing dates? #1348

If you want to enter for an upcoming examination session, please familiarise yourself with the exam entry closing dates.


I'm a joint scheme student, what do I need to do? #1348

As you are resident in a country where we work in partnership with a local accountancy body as part of a Joint Education Scheme, you must re-register in accordance with the terms of the joint scheme. Find contact details of your local Joint examination scheme.


Are there any updates to the terms and conditions since I joined? #1348

Since you joined, there may have been some updates to our terms and conditions. We ask that you review our online declaration that sets these out and which you agree to comply with as a student. In particular, if you’ve not already done so, please promptly notify us in writing if you’ve been, or subsequently become, subject to any matters which may engage Bye-law 8.


Do I need to re-register as a joint scheme student if I was previously registered as part of a joint scheme? #1349

Yes, if you're resident in a country where ACCA operates as part of a Joint Examination Scheme, you must re-register in accordance with the terms of the joint scheme. You can find out how to re-register with your joint scheme and download the re-registration form on our website. You can also view the contact details for our Joint Examination Partnerships there.


What should I do if I was removed for non-payment of JES fees or ACCA fees? #1349

If you were removed for non-payment of JES fees or ACCA fees, you may need to pay your outstanding fees with the JES. The JES will then email ACCA to confirm your re-registration and you will be able to call Connect and re-register over the phone.


Will I get back my passes/exemptions when I re-register? #1315

Yes, you will retain your previous results, except for Professional level exams that may be expired due to the 7-year rule.


What if I can't find my registration number? #1315

Try searching for the account using the client's name or email address. If you still can't find it, contact Student Support for help.


What happens to my results if I completed the old syllabus? #1315

If you're no longer on file, you may not receive credit for all papers due to syllabus changes and time requirements. Contact Student Support to retrieve your records and confirm if any results can be converted to the current syllabus.


Will my previously assessed qualifications be re-assessed upon re-registration? #1315

No, previously assessed qualifications will not be re-assessed upon re-registration.


What is the status of my temporary re-activation request? #1349

Your myACCA account has been reopened, and we request you log in and pay the fees within 10 days.


What should I do if I forget my myACCA password? #1349

If you forget your password, you can click on "Forgotten your password" on the myACCA login page, enter your username, and follow the steps to reset your password.


What are the password requirements for myACCA? #1349

Your new password must be at least eight characters long and contain one lowercase alphabetic character, one special character (e.g., ? !/ # ~@), one uppercase alphabetic character, and one numeric character.


What will happen if I don't pay the fees within 10 days? #1349

If payment is not made within 10 days, your account will be re-suspended. This will not incur any further penalties, but you will still owe the same amount if you try to re-register within the same calendar year.


I received an email about a duplicate account. What should I do? #1354

You have another student account with us that is currently suspended. To continue your studies, you need to pay the outstanding balance and re-registration fee. Since you've already paid part of it, you only need to pay the remaining amount. Please confirm if you wish to proceed, and we'll arrange for your account to be re-registered.


How much do I need to pay to re-register my account? #1354

You need to pay the outstanding balance and re-registration fee. If you've already paid part of it, you only need to pay the remaining amount. The exact amount depends on your account status, which we can check if you provide your registration number. Please note that you have 10 working days to make payment upon re-registration.


Can I make payment online after re-registration? #1354

Yes, once your account is re-registered, you'll be able to access your myACCA account again and make payments online. We'll apply the outstanding fees to your account, and you'll have 10 working days to make payment. If you fail to make payment, your account will be resuspended.


What is the cost to re-register via the Joint Scheme? #1355

No payments made to Joint Schemes are reflected in myACCA. Please contact your local Joint Scheme for confirmation of any fees due to them.


What should I do if I owe fees to my Joint Scheme? #1355

Please arrange payment to your Joint Scheme. Once payment is made, they will send us an email confirming your account is up to date with them. You'll receive a reference number for this email, at which point you can call ACCA Connect at +44 141 582 2000 and quote this number. After some checks, we’ll then be able to proceed with your re-registration request.


What fees do I need to pay to ACCA for re-registration? #1355

You'll need to pay a re-registration fee (same as the initial registration fee) to us, together with any fees that were outstanding when you left the register.


What is my username and password for myACCA? #1614

Your username is your registration number and your password remains the same as before.


What should I do if I forgot my myACCA password? #1614

Answer: Go to the forgotten password section on myACCA, answer the personal information questions, and a new password will be emailed to you within two hours.


Can I book for any of the upcoming exam sessions? #1614

Yes, you can now book your exams again through the exam planner on your myACCA account.


Where can I find the exam entry deadlines and fees and charges? #1614

You can view the exam entry deadlines and fees and charges on the ACCA website under the exam planner and price list sections.


What resources are available to help me prepare for my exams? #1614

There is a wide range of resources available to help you prepare for your exams, such as accessing the syllabus and study guide, gaining insights from the Examining Team, or broadening your understanding of exam topics. Please see our exam resources for more information.
