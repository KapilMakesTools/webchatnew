ACCA Exchange Employers or Approved Partners

If you work for an ACCA exchange employer or approved partner, please contact your company first before re-registering with us.


Residents of Joint Scheme Countries

If you live in a country where ACCA works in partnership with a local accountancy body, you need to re-register with them first and they will forward your re-registration request to us. Please read the information for residents of joint scheme countries before proceeding.

If you're ready to re-register and pay with a debit/credit card, please call ACCA Connect at +44 (0) 141 582 2000. You will need to provide your ACCA registration number, email address, mailing address, and a valid credit/debit card in your name. Once the payment is made, your account will be re-activated immediately, and you can access myACCA and book exams again. Alternatively you can do bank transfer using the details here or we can re-activate your myACCA account temporarily for you to make the payment.

Registration

Registration Process for ACCA

You can register online at any time of the year to become an ACCA student. Applying online allows you to upload all your supporting documents, ensuring a faster application process. Remember to include all supporting documents at the time of registration to avoid delays.


Entry Requirements for ACCA Qualification

The minimum entry requirements for the ACCA Qualification are two A-Levels and three GCSEs (or their equivalent) in five separate subjects, including English and maths. Check if your qualifications meet the minimum entry requirements by visiting the minimum entrance requirements database.


Entry Requirements for Foundation-level

There are no minimum entry requirements to start at any level. Still, it's recommended to start with the ACCA Diploma in Financial and Management Accounting (RQF Level 2) if you have no formal qualifications. This will help you perform better in higher-level exams.


Documents Required for Registration

You must provide documents supporting your educational achievements, proof of identity, and translations of your documents (if they are not in English). Official translations must be stamped by a licensed translator. You can upload all your documents during the online application process.


Registration Process Timeline

The online application should take no more than 10 minutes to complete, and you should be registered as a student within 5–7 working days if you complete the application fully online and provide all the required documents. If you have to send documents or payment by post, you should be registered within 10 working days.


Fees Payable

A subscription fee is charged in addition to the initial registration fee in your first year as an ACCA student if you are eligible to sit at two or more exam sessions in a calendar year. Thereafter, the annual subscription fee is due and payable on or before 1 January each year you wish to remain an active student. Tuition fees are separate and paid directly to the tuition provider. For more information on exam entry fees, visit the apply now page in the Related Links.


Payment Required for Registration

To finalise your registration for remote session exams, you need to pay the relevant fee. Until then, you won't receive official confirmation and won't be able to attempt any exams. We advise against booking tuition until your registration is confirmed.

Exemptions

Exemptions Overview

If you already have certain qualifications, you may be eligible for exemptions from some of our exams. However, please note that exemptions are not awarded until after your education documentation has been assessed and your registration application completed. For more information, please visit our exemption FAQs.


Exemptions Calculator

You can use our Exemptions calculator to get an idea of what exemptions you might receive based on the qualifications you already have. However, please note that exemptions shown on exemptions calculator are not guranteed as they provide a preliminaru assessmen and will be confirmed only after your education documentation has been assessed by our exemptions team.


Exemption Assessment

Your exemption eligibility will be assessed as part of your online application. Please ensure that all necessary information is provided and that your education documentation is up-to-date. Exemptions are not awarded until after your education documentation has been assessed and your registration application completed.


Exemption Confirmation

If you are awarded exemptions, you will receive confirmation of these along with your registration confirmation. Please note that exemptions are not awarded until after your education documentation has been assessed and your registration application completed.

Remote Session Exams

Scrap Paper

For exam security, scrap paper is not allowed on the desk during remote session exams. You can use the digital scratchpad provided within the exam platform to make rough notes instead.


Exam Security

Remote session CBEs are remotely invigilated with students being viewed on screen by an invigilator at all times. We also implement various exam security measures to maintain the rigour and integrity of the exams.


Zoom Text Tool for Vision Impaired Students

The Zoom Text tool is only available on devices using a Windows operating system that meet the minimum technical requirements. Unfortunately, it is not available on MAC devices. Vision impaired students authorized to use the tool should keep this in mind before the exam.
